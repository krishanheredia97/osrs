<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Merchant" redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/w/Merchant_(disambiguation)" title="Merchant (disambiguation)">Merchant (disambiguation)</a>.  </div>
<figure class="mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File"><a href="/w/File:Coins_detail.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="/images/thumb/Coins_detail.png/120px-Coins_detail.png?404bc" decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="120" height="118" class="mw-file-element" srcset="/images/thumb/Coins_detail.png/240px-Coins_detail.png?404bc 2x" data-file-width="924" data-file-height="911"></a><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <p><b>Merchanting</b> or <b>merching</b>, is the act of purchasing an <a href="/w/Item" class="mw-redirect" title="Item">item</a> at a low price and then selling that item at a higher price to generate profit. It is a popular method used by wealthy players to make <a href="/w/Money" class="mw-redirect" title="Money">money</a>.
</p><p>With the addition of the <a href="/w/Grand_Exchange" title="Grand Exchange">Grand Exchange</a>, it is no longer simple for merchants to act as an intermediary between players who seek to easily sell and players who seek to easily buy — the Grand Exchange replaced this. Modern merchants, however, have found ways to work with and around the Grand Exchange to make money, including long-term investments, time-based arbitrage, price manipulation, "<a href="/w/Market_value" class="mw-redirect" title="Market value">market value</a>" to "<a href="/w/Street_value" class="mw-redirect" title="Street value">street value</a>" arbitrage, and item conversion.
</p>

<h2 class="mw-header"><a id="Types_of_merchanting" href="#Types_of_merchanting" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Types of merchanting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=1&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Types of merchanting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section's source code: Types of merchanting">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Players can make money through several methods. Some are shown below.
</p>
<h3 class="mw-header"><a id="Investing" href="#Investing" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Investing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=2&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Investing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section's source code: Investing">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Synonymous with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speculation" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Speculation">speculation</a>, this practice involves predicting an item's long-term price trend and then acting upon it. A player will buy as much as possible of an item when they think it is at a low point and sell it when they think it is reaching a high point. To make money with this method, players must accurately make a prediction as to how a future or current game <a href="/w/Update" class="mw-redirect" title="Update">update</a> or other in-game event will affect item prices in the future. Investing is more profitable when the item has large changes in price and safer when these changes are predictable.
</p>
<h3 class="mw-header"><a id="Flipping" href="#Flipping" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Flipping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=3&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Flipping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section's source code: Flipping">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This involves buying items and then selling them at a slightly higher price in the short term, often immediately after buying or even while buying. The main difference from investing, which relies on price changes over time, is that flipping exploits the spread between pending buy orders and pending sell orders as the source of profit. The buy-sell spread is created by the diversity of buyers and sellers with varying degrees of patience. Flipping is more profitable when the item can be bought and sold within a large price range, and safer when the item has a high trade volume.
</p>
<h3 class="mw-header"><a id="Bulk_quantity_flipping" href="#Bulk_quantity_flipping" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Bulk quantity flipping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=4&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Bulk quantity flipping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section's source code: Bulk quantity flipping">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This involves buying large quantities of an item with a stable price range of a few coins and then selling that item for a slightly higher price. The profit on each item is very small, often only a couple of gold each, but the massive quantity results in large profits. Large amounts of available cash are often required to perform bulk quantity flipping, but can result in a higher profit than other merchanting methods. When bulk flipping, 20 million or more is almost surely needed to make money faster than possible at common high-level training areas.
</p>
<h3 class="mw-header"><a id="Street/forum_merchanting" href="#Street/forum_merchanting" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Street/forum merchanting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=5&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Street/forum merchanting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section's source code: Street/forum merchanting">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This method involves buying a bulk of an item and then selling it on the street or <a href="/w/Forums" class="mw-redirect" title="Forums">forums</a> for a higher price. There are some items that have a buy limit that is below the demand level of a player. For example, <a href="/w/Dragon_dart_tip" title="Dragon dart tip">dragon dart tips</a> have a buy limit of 11,000, but more than that can be used in four hours of Fletching training. Players can take advantage of this by placing a quantity of offers over a period of time then finding a buyer on the forums or on the streets willing to pay a higher price to buy more than the buy limit at once.
</p>
<h2 class="mw-header"><a id="How_the_Grand_Exchange_works" href="#How_the_Grand_Exchange_works" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">How the Grand Exchange works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=6&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: How the Grand Exchange works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section's source code: How the Grand Exchange works">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Understanding the <a href="/w/Grand_Exchange" title="Grand Exchange">Grand Exchange</a> is important to trading.
</p><p>If an offer is placed in the Grand Exchange, the game will search for all offers meeting your price or better, and instantly completes those trades, using the price of the already existing offer. If an offer is not instantly completed, it will remain until a matching offer is entered or it is cancelled. Thus, all trades occurring in the Grand Exchange occur instantly for one person and not instantly for the other.
</p><p><a href="/w/Free_players" class="mw-redirect" title="Free players">Free players</a> can only have a maximum of three offers in the Grand Exchange at any time, while <a href="/w/Members" title="Members">members</a> can have a maximum of eight offers.
</p><p>Most items have a maximum quantity that can be bought every four hours. This limit was implemented by <a href="/w/Jagex" title="Jagex">Jagex</a> to prevent price manipulation. A full list of the maximum purchases can be seen <a href="/w/Grand_Exchange/Buying_limits" title="Grand Exchange/Buying limits">here</a>.
</p><p>A common misconception about the Grand Exchange is attaching special meaning to the <a href="/w/Guide_Price" class="mw-redirect" title="Guide Price">Guide Price</a>. In reality, the guide price is the average price of <i>yesterday's</i> trades (provided that it is not more than 5% different from yesterday's guide price) and may no longer be relevant.
</p><p>In general, items are always rising in price or falling in price. If an item is rising in price, an attempt to sell it for guide price will be instantly completed, and an attempt to buy for the guide price will not be instantly completed. The opposite is true for items falling in price.
</p>
<h2 class="mw-header"><a id="Determining_prices" href="#Determining_prices" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Determining prices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=7&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Determining prices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section's source code: Determining prices">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of the most important factors in successfully profiting from trade is being able to properly determine the <a href="/w/Price" class="mw-redirect" title="Price">price</a> of an item.
</p><p>There are two values that must be considered in every trade: the <a href="/w/Market_value" class="mw-redirect" title="Market value">market value</a> and the <a href="/w/Street_value" class="mw-redirect" title="Street value">street value</a>. The market value is the price that an item can be bought or sold for in the <a href="/w/Grand_Exchange" title="Grand Exchange">Grand Exchange</a>, whereas the street value is the price that an item can be bought or sold for in person, often through the <a href="/w/Forums" class="mw-redirect" title="Forums">forums</a>.
</p>
<h3 class="mw-header"><a id="Buy/sell_test" href="#Buy/sell_test" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Buy/sell test</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=8&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Buy/sell test">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section's source code: Buy/sell test">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The buy-sell test is an important tool used to determine the <a href="/w/Market_value" class="mw-redirect" title="Market value">market value</a> of an item in the <a href="/w/Grand_Exchange" title="Grand Exchange">Grand Exchange</a>. The price at which an item is trading for in the Grand Exchange may differ slightly or even greatly from the guide price. In order to determine the market value of an item, we first put in a buy offer for one quantity of it at considerably above the guide price. The amount that it buys for is the instant-buy price (high end of the price range). Next, we offer to sell that one quantity of the item for considerably below the guide price. The amount that it sells for is the instant-sell price (low end of the price range). So, if you bought the item for 135 coins and sold it for 130 coins, then its market value is anywhere between 130 coins and 135 coins.
</p><p><b><span class="template-colour" style="color:red;">Warning</span>:</b> The buy-sell test is not suitable for expensive items and may result in a substantial loss of wealth.
</p>
<h3 class="mw-header"><a id="Street_value" href="#Street_value" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Street value</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=9&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Street value">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section's source code: Street value">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For several reasons, items often have <a href="/w/Street_value" class="mw-redirect" title="Street value">street values</a> (prices at which they are traded in person) that are different from that item's <a href="/w/Market_value" class="mw-redirect" title="Market value">market value</a>. Those reasons include:
</p>
<ul><li>The item is hard to obtain (e.g. <a href="/w/3rd_age_equipment" title="3rd age equipment">3rd age equipment</a>).</li>
<li>Investors desire large quantities that cannot be obtained through the <a href="/w/Grand_Exchange" title="Grand Exchange">Grand Exchange</a>.</li>
<li>People may feel the item's value is not being properly represented by the Grand Exchange.</li></ul>
<p>People often trade items at these street values in trade <a href="/w/Worlds" class="mw-redirect" title="Worlds">worlds</a>, or arrange trades on the <a href="/w/Forums" class="mw-redirect" title="Forums">forums</a>. By checking other players' offers at these locations, one can find wider price ranges and therefore generate more profit. As an example, if the market value of an item is 120 coins to 125 coins each, but a buyer offers to pay 127 coins each on the forums, additional profit can be made by buying that item from the Grand Exchange and selling it to the buyer on the forums.
</p><p>In almost all cases, it can be guaranteed that the street value of an item is higher than the market value. This is because if the instant-sell value in the Grand Exchange is higher than the street value, suppliers will just sell to the Grand Exchange instead. However, it is only guaranteed that the street value of an item is higher than the market value if the item is actually even traded on the street. If you intend to rely on street values to make money, make sure that the relevant items are traded on the street first.
</p>
<h2 class="mw-header"><a id="Investing_2" href="#Investing_2" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Investing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=10&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Investing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section's source code: Investing">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The two most important elements of investing are: to be aware of prices and price trends, and also how recent <a href="/w/Updates" title="Updates">updates</a> or general trends in <i><a href="/w/Old_School_RuneScape" title="Old School RuneScape">Old School RuneScape</a></i> are affecting the market. Following are some investment strategies. However, as with all investments, there is never an absolute guarantee for profit or loss.
</p><p>Carefully watch the price of an item, and buy it when the price is decreasing but starting to bottom out. Ideally, you would sell while the item is still going up in price, the day before the price reverses and item starts going down in price. However, it is not always easy to predict exactly when this will be. Players may want to explore the exchange graphs on the item's page to examine price trends. Players may also find the <a href="/w/Grand_Exchange_Market_Watch" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Exchange Market Watch">Grand Exchange Market Watch</a> helpful. Examining the graph an item's <a href="/w/Market_price" class="mw-redirect" title="Market price">market price</a> may show that it always stays within a certain price range, while moving back and forth. This makes it safer to buy at the low end of that price range and sell at the high end.
</p><p>Reading the <a href="/w/Behind_the_Scenes" title="Behind the Scenes">Behind the Scenes</a> and other updates can give a hint of what items may become high demand in the future. However, be cautious of the fact that if enough players think likewise, they may also stockpile large quantities of that item, resulting in a large surplus and a drop in value when the update occurs.
</p><p><a href="/w/Jagex" title="Jagex">Jagex</a> often releases content in advance of an update so that the price will be more stable when the update occurs. A classic example of this is the release of <a href="/w/Teak_logs" title="Teak logs">teak</a> and <a href="/w/Mahogany_logs" title="Mahogany logs">mahogany logs</a> before the release of the <a href="/w/Construction" title="Construction">Construction</a> skill, which made significant use of both. When Construction was released, investors already held a decent supply of teak and mahogany logs to meet the demand of players training the new Construction skill.
</p>
<h2 class="mw-header"><a id="Flipping_2" href="#Flipping_2" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Flipping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=11&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Flipping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section's source code: Flipping">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Flipping involves buying an item for as little as possible and then selling for as much as possible within a few hours. Flipping is unique in that it can be used to profit from any item, regardless of its price trend. Flipping is only recommended for players with plenty of starting cash (10M at least) to do it, as not doing so will result in low profits.
</p><p>Remember that it can be difficult to buy an item for below <a href="/w/Guide_Price" class="mw-redirect" title="Guide Price">Guide Price</a> and sell for above the guide price, unless the price actually changes from falling to rising, because prices are virtually always either falling or rising, and often only switch every few days.
</p><p>To determine the buy-sell range, buy one item at considerably above the market price. The amount it buys for is the high price (the highest price the item can be sold for). Now, sell that one item at well below the market price. The amount it sells for is the low price (the lowest price the item can be bought for). If the item cannot be bought or sold at a significant margin, it is unsuitable for flipping.
</p><p>If you buy an item and find that a major crash has started and you cannot sell at a high price, you can either hang on to it until the price rises again, or dump it at a low price. If you choose your items carefully, this shouldn't happen very often. A wise rule is to always do a test buy of a small quantity, and then resell to see how quickly the item moves. If you can buy ten easily, then sell ten easily; next time, try to buy 50/sell 50, then try to buy 250/sell 250. Never start out buying a huge quantity of an untested item. If you have extra money when a price drop begins on an item you have already purchased in bulk, you can also put another buy order in at a lower price to reduce the average price you pay per item across all purchases, and prevent a loss in that way.
</p><p>You also want to find items that are in your price range. If you have only 100,000 coins to invest on an item, you probably don't want to pick an item that is 50,000 coins as you would only be able to buy two of them. Instead, pick items that are about 250-500 coins, that way you could buy up to 200-400 of them. <i>Quantity is usually more important than price.</i> The key is profit per hour. If you can move more items quicker, you might make more money even though it's a smaller profit on each individual item. Ever wonder why stores have big sales? This is why.
</p><p>Another important concept is diversification. <i>Do not put all your money on one item.</i> If it drops in price, you are in trouble. Try to pick four to six good items (you may have to experiment to find four good ones) and just keep buying low, and selling high. Remember there are always impatient players who get rare items as a drop, such as <a href="/w/Dragon_equipment" title="Dragon equipment">dragon items</a>, and they need cash fast, so they will sell for a low price. Then, later will come the impatient buyer, who has to have that item right away and is willing to pay a lot of money. Patience is one of the keys to making money in trading.
</p>

<h2 class="mw-header"><a id="Tips" href="#Tips" title="Link to this section" class="mw-headline-headanchor"></a><span class="mw-headline">Tips</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/Merchanting?section=13&amp;veaction=edit" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Tips">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/w/Merchanting?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section's source code: Tips">edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>It is strongly recommended to not flip static-price items. Instead, always try to search for dynamic-price items.</li>
<li>Merchants should keep a record of their purchases and prices.</li>
<li>Patience is a virtue especially when it comes to merchanting.</li></ul>
